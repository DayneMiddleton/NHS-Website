Nourshing Drinks

Nourshing drinks

If your mouth is too sore or you are having difficulty chewing or swallowing you may need to follow a liquid diet.

Depending on your swallowing difficulties you may need further guidance from a Speech and Language therapist. Discuss this with your medical team.

Here are a few examples of information on how to have nourshing liquids when you need a liquidised diet. If you have recently lost weight, you will also find some useful ideas on how to add extra goodness to your daily drinks.

Nourshing Savoury Drinks

You can convery normal meals into fluids by using a liquidiser , food processor or a hand blender.

Before blending cut food up into small pieces and remove the skin, gristle and fat from meat.

    Suitable meals to blend include:

  • Hot-pots
  • Stews
  • Casseroles
  • Curries
  • Minced meat.
  • Corned beef hash
  • Shephards pie
  • Cottage pie

Liquid foods can be very bland so consider including:

  • Tomato sauce
  • Herbs and spices
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • Mustard
  • Chutney
  • Lemon juice

It may be easier to liquidise meals in bulk and then freeze in portions, defrosting and reheating as needed.

Other suitable drinks

Make sure that all liquids are smooth before drinking

  • Yoghurt shakes
  • Vegetable juice
  • Smooth soups
  • Fruit juice ( Try to have a glass of fruit juice daily to provide vitamin C )
